Themes in a book tend to encapsulate the main characters journey. In life, you are your own main character and your life is your journey and it needs a theme.

Your theme does not need to be new or groundbreaking. As you’ll see my themes come from quotes or ideas I’ve read.

I try to keep my theme at the forefront of my thinking as I go through the new year. It’s a sticker on the cover of my journal so I see it everyday.

## 2022 Theme: Verbs are Greater than Nouns.
The idea is a fairly simple one stolen from Austin Kleon. He writes,

“Let go of the thing that you’re trying to be, the noun and focus on the actual work you need to be doing the verb”

In life I applied this in many ways, for example:

- I want to be a runner, so I go running a lot.
- I want to be a Software Engineer IV, so I write a lot code.
- I want to be a reader, so I spend a lot of time reading

## 2023 Theme: Consistency over Perfection
When looking at a new project or idea, I want to think about how can I do this work consistently. Some days will be better than others, but if I show up everyday, then I will have a greater chance of success in the long run.

James Clear, author and habit guru, said,

> “Too often we fall into an all-or-nothing cycle with our habits. The problem is not slipping up; the problem is thinking that if you can’t do something perfectly then you shouldn’t do it at all.”

My plan for the year is to show up each day — as a dad, as a husband, as an engineer, as a runner— in as many aspects of my life as I can.

Will everyday be perfect of course not. I’ve logged many terrible runs and have been less then a stellar husband at times. But if I show up each day and try to do good on the day then hopefully it will all workout in the end.

Recently I read a quote from Phil Stutz, Jonah Hill’s therapist and subject of the documentary Stutz. He said,

> “True confidence is sitting in uncertainty and moving forward. The winner is the one who is willing to take the risk to work the cycle. All I have to worry about is forward motion and putting the next pearl on the string.”

John Steinbeck, called this doing the daily stint. That’s it everyday, in every project, in every moment do the daily work required. Some days it will be perfect and others will be less then, but each day you tick the box you are one step closer. Consistency over perfection.

That’s my annual theme what will be yours?
